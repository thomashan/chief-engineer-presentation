Does everyone knows what architecture is?

An aspiring developer want to be an architect...

Here's a conversation of the aspiring developer (A) with Uncle Bob (B) http://blog.cleancoder.com/uncle-bob/2016/01/04/ALittleArchitecture.html

A: I want to become a Software Architect.

B: That’s a fine goal for a young software developer.

A: I want to lead a team and make all the important decisions about databases and frameworks and web-servers and all that stuff.

B: Oh. Well, then you don’t want to become a Software Architect after all.

A: Of course I do! I want to be the one who makes all the important decisions.

B: That’s fine, but you didn’t list the important decisions. You listed the irrelevant ones.

A: What do you mean? The Database isn’t an important decision? Do you know how much money we spend on them?

B: Too much probably. And, no; the database is not one of the most important decisions. ... The database is merely an IO device. It happens to provide some useful tools for sorting, querying, and reporting but those are ancillary to the system architecture.

How can databases not be important to the architecture?!

Misconceptions of what architecture is.

It's not about choosing the right

  • database
  • framework
  • web server
  • delivery mechanism (e.g. web, mobile, desktop)

because the business rules do not depend upon the tools of the database, nor the framework, nor the web server, nor how it's delivered.

You're talking gibberish.

Am I?

On the contrary, it's the fundamentals of software architecture!

I didn't say don't use those tools. I said business rules shouldn’t depend upon them.

Business rules (highest level policies) shouldn't depend upon low level tools.

This is called the Dependency Inversion Principle or the Inversion of Control principal.

Architecture misconceptions everywhere!

Enterprises spend millions of dollars and months/years to upgrade a database.

And what business value did the upgrade bring?

I'm not saying don't upgrade the database, I'm asking whether millions of dollars and thousands of man hours is warranted to upgrade a database system.

Architecture misconceptions everywhere!

What is clean architecture?

To me it's about:

  • ability to decide on the outer circles later.
  • testability - run use cases without any framework (frameworks are tools as well!)
    • Integrated tests are a scam
  • rapid feedback between dev/test cycles
  • domain model should scream about what the application does
  • avoiding stored procedures in the database

How?

Be bold for change. Lots of courage!

Many clients think they know what architecture is.

  • This is hard to change because
    • we have to change the mindsets of many architects
    • shifts in both mindsets and technology stack is required
    • many clients focus on the irrelevant things (e.g. database, delivery mechanism)

How do we get there?

Most importantly,

  • do the right think, and be brutally honest about it
  • make sure we're building the right thing before we build it right
  • engineers should be removed from all the politics and bureaucracy to have these honest discussions
